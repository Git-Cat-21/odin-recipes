Lemon Juice

Lemon juice is a tangy, refreshing liquid extracted from the fruit of the lemon tree. It has a sharp, acidic flavor and is rich in vitamin C, making it a popular ingredient in both culinary and beverage preparations. Lemon juice is commonly used to enhance the taste of dishes, from salads and marinades to desserts and beverages. It is also used for its preservative qualities and its ability to add a bright, zesty flavor to recipes. Additionally, lemon juice is a popular natural remedy and is often used in home cleaning solutions due to its antibacterial properties.

Ingredients

  • Lemons: 4-6 medium-sized lemons
  • Water: 4 cups
  • Sugar: 1/2 to 3/4 cup
  • Ice Cubes: As needed
  • Mint Leaves: For garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Lemons: Roll the lemons on a countertop to soften them, which helps to release more juice. Cut the lemons in half.
  2. Extract the Juice: Use a lemon squeezer to extract the juice from each lemon.
  3. Dissolve the Sugar: In a separate small pot, combine the sugar with 1 cup of water.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Add the simple syrup to the pitcher of lemon juice. Add the remaining 3 cups of cold water to the pitcher. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  5. Serve: Fill glasses with ice cubes. Pour the lemon juice over the ice. Garnish with fresh mint leaves if desired.
